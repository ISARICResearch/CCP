Clinical Characterisation Protocol Global Toolkit

The Clinical Characterisation Protocol (CCP) is designed for any severe or potentially severe acute infection of public health interest. It is a standardised protocol that enables data and biological samples to be collected rapidly in a globally-harmonised manner.

ISARIC offers a web-based eCRF for the CCP and can host the data. For further information please contact data@isaric.org.

The resources are free to use. ISARIC supports researchers to retain control of the data and samples they collect.

Steps for operationalisation of CCP

This page provides an overview of the main steps for implementation of tiers 1, 2 and 3. It is important that the study is not seen as a separate activity in the facility’s response strategy to the infection of interest.

A Principal Investigator must be appointed; They will be responsible for all aspects of the study, following the main steps outlined below.

ISARIC CCP

Steps for Operationalisation of CCP

Adapt and amend the Clinical Characterisation Protocol (CCP) to local context/capacity.

Conduct a review to assess the resources required to carry out the CCP locally.

Depending on local resources, interest and feasibility, chose which Tier to use. You can choose to change tier at a later date; The patient data recorded is the same for all tiers.

Review ‘Site Capacity: Points to consider’, to assist with determining the tier level for your site. Organise a Study Master File (electronic/paper). A file which contains key documents and procedures.

Site capacity checklist

  • Tier 0: Patient data (CRF) only.
  • Tier 1: Patient data (CRF) + Single Biological Sample.
  • Tier 2: Patient data (CRF) + Serial Biological Sampling.
  • Tier 3: Patient data (CRF) + Population Pharmacokinetics of antimicrobials & immune modulators.
  • How to operationalise the CCP
  • Tier and sub-study record template

Organise a Study Master File (electronic/paper).

Develop standard operational procedures (SOPs) to be used by the study team.

Ensure staff are trained in research (collecting patient data, processing and labelling samples).

Ensure staff have access to Personal Protection Equipment (PPE) for delivering research.
