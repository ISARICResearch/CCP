Clinical Characterisation Protocol Global Toolkit

The Clinical Characterisation Protocol (CCP) is designed for any severe or potentially severe acute infection of public health interest. It is a standardised protocol that enables data and biological samples to be collected rapidly in a globally-harmonised manner.

ISARIC has partnered with global experts to adapt its Clinical Epidemiology Platform, enabling research on the clinical characteristics of emerging infectious disease outbreaks to guide patient care and inform health policy. Dedicated to enhancing evidence generation, ISARIC provides tools and support for research teams to quickly set up studies address the research questions in their context and inform clinical trial design. The platform is also available for collaborative analyses that can characterise disease outbreaks across different variants, countries, and over time.

The ISARIC-WHO Clinical Characterisation Protocol and the ISARIC Clinical Epidemiology Platform is a suite of openly-available, standardised tools to support your research and facilitate global collaboration. Tools within this platform will help you to create a Case Report Form (CRF), launch a database, and perform statistical analysis on the data you collect.The resources are free to use. ISARIC provides support to implement them and supports researchers to retain control of the data and samples they collect.

Protocol

The ISARIC-WHO Clinical Characterisation Protocol (CCP) provides a standardised method for the rapid, coordinated clinical investigation of emerging health threats to inform clinical care, public health response, and clinical trial design. This protocol can be tailored to fit any context. Researchers may add additional research questions, customise the procedures to their sites, and alter the investigations according to their priorities and available resources. Several sites have used the CCP to create their own regionally-specific versions for specific response activities. Examples are available at the links below.
